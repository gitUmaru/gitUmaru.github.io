3D Printed Prosthetic LimbComplete

My friend and I had designed and built a 3d printed prosthetic limb that was controlled by the user's muscle input via an EMG. The raw frequency data was taken in as an analog value and coordinated with several servos which would pull on fishing wire to move the arm. This project won us: Silver in the South fraser Regional Science Fair 2019, Engineers and Geoscientists of BC Award, and BC Game Developers and Innovation Award

Model Maglev TrainComplete

One of my close friend and I had decided to build a magnetic railway to propell a payload with ferrous magnets attached. The railway consisted of alternating polarity of solenoids (as to change directions via computer systems). Furthermore, I had written a piece of software that would detect for colour using a computer vision library; this way I could scan for derailments.
